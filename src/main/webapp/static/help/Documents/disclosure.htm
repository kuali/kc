Business Needs and Purpose

The core business requirement is to assure that institutions are in compliance with all federal, state, and institutional regulations relating to conflict of interest.

The Disclosure document is completed whenever:

•      There is a potential conflict of interest

•      There is a need to disclose the potential conflict of interest internally

The Disclosure document is used to:

•      Report and maintain an Annual disclosure to satisfy a COI office request

•      Report and maintain a new Event-Based disclosure (previously-undisclosed, or change to previously-disclosed)

Policy

The policy statements that pertain to this e-doc and it’s related activities are:

•      When a new human or animal subjects protocol is created, a disclosure is required

•      When a proposal is submitted for extramural support, a disclosure is required

The core federal regulatory requirements addressing Conflicts of Interest are: 

•      Public Health Service:  The review and management of conflict of interest disclosures made by “Investigators” is required under 42 CFR 50 and 45 CFR 94 for projects funded by the Public Health Service.  In order to comply with these regulations, each institution must have in place a process to identify and manage conflicts of interests related to the ongoing research of the reporter.  In addition, the records related to conflicts of interest must be kept and accessible for at least three years after the date of the initial disclosure.

•      National Science Foundation:  The NSF requires each grantee institution employing more than fifty persons to maintain an appropriate written and enforced policy on conflict of interest. An institutional conflict of interest policy should require that each investigator disclose to a responsible representative of the institution all significant disclosable interests of the investigator (including those of the investigator’s spouse and dependent children) (i) that would reasonably appear to be affected by the research or educational activities funded or proposed for funding by NSF; or (ii) in entities whose disclosable interests would reasonably appear to be affected by such activities.

•      Additional Agency COI Requirements:  Additional federal and state agencies may have regulatory requirements regarding disclosing  Conflict of Interest.  It is incumbent upon participating institutions to assure that they are in full compliance with any and all current or future federal, state, or local agency requirements not detailed herein.

•      California Political Reform Act of 1974:  Many states also impose conflict of interest disclosure requirements such as the California Political Reform Act of 1974, which generally exceeds the conflict of interest disclosure requirements imposed by federal agencies.   Each KualiCoeus institution is responsible for discovery of their applicable state rules and regulations related to conflict of interest laws and inclusion of same in their disclosure processes.

Roles and Responsibilities

The primary responsibilities for each actor in this procedure are:

•      The Reporter is responsible for disclosing they have a significant disclosable interest in an outside entity as a result of consulting, employment, equity ownership, or any income-producing relationship.

•      The COI Administrator is responsible for serving as institutional approval signatory of the completed disclosure record submitted by the Reporter with authority to remand to Reporter for clarification.

The generic actor Reporter in this procedure may refer to any the following equivalent applicable job titles:

•      Faculty member

•      Principal Investigator Employee

•      Researcher

•      Student

Completing A Disclosure Document

Introduction

 

You complete the Disclosure E-Doc when you need to create a new disclosure or change an existing one because of a potential conflict of interest related to a sponsored research project.  The general idea is to disclose when personal financial interests of those responsible for the research might benefit from the predictable results of the proposed research projects.

Example Scenarios

Here are some real-world functional business needs for Reporters (Researchers) and how using this e-doc fulfills them:

Institutions have different forms on which Internal Disclosures are made.  For example, an institution may require reporters to complete a report of outside financial interest on an “event based” (aka “transactional”) basis.  This Internal Disclosure would be associated with a particular proposal or an IRB application and ensures that the reporter does not have any conflicts or outside interest that must be disclosed on that particular proposal or IRB protocol. 

Other institutions may require a certain class of users (e.g., all faculty) to complete an “annual” internal disclosure whether or not they engage in human subjects research or apply for outside funding.  Some institutions require both “event based” and “annual” types of disclosures.  The workflow routing for each type of form is different and because they are asked for different purposes, the questions differ from form to form.  Other differences between form types include the scope of individuals who are required to fill out the form, and the time frame that the form is considered “in effect” and available for completion.  For example, some schools have annual disclosure processes that run on annual cycles. 

Here are some real-world functional business needs for COI Administrators and how using this e-doc fulfills them:

After a researcher completes an internal disclosure of a disclosable financial interest and that internal disclosure form has routed through any levels of review required by the institution (for example, to that person’s department chair or dean), it will be routed to a conflict of interest administrator.  The conflict of interest administrator will perform an initial evaluation to determine whether review or actions are required to manage the potential conflict of interest.  An evaluation might conclude that no further action is required, or it may conclude that the internal disclosure needs to be reviewed by the institution’s conflict of interest committee.  Administrators need to be able to search and find internal disclosure as well as information about where the internal disclosure is in the “review” process, and whether a conclusion to the review is complete.

Users who have responsibility for the administration of COI rules at universities need to be able to record the “status” of these steps of review (e.g., “No further action required – not a conflict of interest” or “Pending in front of committee” or “management plan in place”, etc.) so that the COI administrator can record the “status” for each internal disclosure of disclosable financial interests as it moves through the process of that institution.  Other administrative actions may include indicating that there is a relationship between a internal disclosure and a proposal, award, or IRB protocol.  An administrator may also need to return the internal disclosure for additional information in the event that there is insufficient detail in the internal disclosure.
